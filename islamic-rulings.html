Islamic Rulings · Fiqh Research Compilation

What does each field mean for a Muslim student?

For every program family in this explorer: the classical and contemporary scholarly position on studying it, and on working in it — sourced from the Qur'an, Sunnah, and named fatwa bodies, using the five traditional rulings.

Read this first: this page is a research compilation, not a fatwa. It summarises what recognised scholars, fiqh academies, and established fatwa institutions have actually said — with citations — rather than presenting an original ruling. Where genuine scholarly disagreement exists (most notably on psychology and on practising secular law), both positions are presented rather than one being picked as final. Fiqh rulings depend heavily on specific intention and circumstance — for a binding personal ruling on your own situation, consult a qualified local scholar.

Methodology

Every Qur'an reference is given by sūrah:āyah so you can read the full verse yourself; every hadith is given with its source collection and number; every fatwa source is linked directly. Full methodology and the complete source list are on the About page.
